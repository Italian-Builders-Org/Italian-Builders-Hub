# What The Community Wants

Source: `X Italian Builders - Mappatura community (Responses)`, tab `Form Responses 1`, 100 submissions, imported as `Community Form`.

## Current Direction

The community is not only an audience for content. The strongest signal is a practical builder network: people want to meet other Italian builders, show what they are building, get feedback, find collaborators, and turn projects into users, clients, or revenue.

The current waitlist is useful for a manual invite rollout because it already contains names, emails, role, current project type, X handle, and source. The highest-value first batch is likely people with active projects, MVPs, revenue, or strong willingness to give feedback or organize formats.

## Who Answered

- 30 developer / engineer
- 26 founder / entrepreneur
- 16 hybrid / other roles
- 9 student / aspiring builder
- 8 data / AI / machine learning
- 5 designer / UX / UI

Project state is mixed but action-oriented:

- 23 are building an MVP

- 10 have a launched product with active users
- 9 report initial revenue
- 10 are still at idea / validation stage

## What People Expect

- Meet other Italian builders: 90
- Learn from people ahead of them: 61
- Receive feedback on their projects: 52
- Local events / meetups: 44
- A public builder directory: 42
- Find collaborators or co-founders: 40
- Share progress and accountability: 35
- Find users, clients, or early adopters: 34
- Discover work or consulting opportunities: 31

The practical goal is not just networking. The top six-month goal is revenue growth, followed by finding users/customers, finding co-founders/collaborators, launching MVPs, and increasing visibility on X.

## Formats They Would Join

- Local meetups in person: 61
- Product feedback sessions: 48
- Online demo days: 42
- AMA with founders, investors, or operators: 41
- Small masterminds: 34
- Practical expert workshops: 33
- Weekly digest with updates and opportunities: 28
- Online sprint / coworking: 24
- Public member directory: 24

The format mix points to a rhythm: directory for discovery, async digest for continuity, and recurring live sessions for feedback, demos, and local connection.

## Topics They Want

- AI / LLM / agents: 81
- Software development / architecture / APIs: 53
- Growth, marketing, and distribution: 50
- Product discovery and validation: 49
- Sales, pricing, and B2B customers: 36
- Fundraising / angels / VC: 34
- Hiring, career, and remote work: 29
- UX, design, and research: 27

AI is the largest topic, but the next layer is commercial: distribution, validation, sales, pricing, and revenue. The community should avoid becoming only an AI chat; people want practical product and business execution.

## Platform Preference

- Telegram: 47
- Discord: 23
- No preference, content matters: 19
- WhatsApp with better rules / threads: 4
- Slack: 3

Telegram is the safest default for the first migration away from WhatsApp. Discord has a meaningful minority, but the bigger lesson is that structure matters more than tool choice for many people.

## Public Profiles

- 36 want a complete public profile
- 12 want a basic public profile
- 43 want to see how the profile is presented before deciding
- 6 prefer not to appear publicly

Profile creation should be opt-in and previewable. The directory should support profile visibility levels instead of assuming everyone wants the same public surface.

## What's Missing

- A source-aware waitlist and invite workflow, so imported leads can be separated from website signups and invited in batches.
- A lightweight member directory with preview/consent controls: full profile, basic profile, private/no public profile.
- A manual review queue with segments: active project, MVP, launched/revenue, wants feedback, can mentor, can organize.
- Local meetup infrastructure: city/region tags, local host volunteers, event calendar, and follow-up notes.
- Feedback/demo programming: weekly or biweekly product feedback sessions, demo days, and small mastermind groups.
- Matching surfaces: co-founder/collaborator matching, early adopter/customer asks, consulting/job opportunities.
- Practical playbooks around B2B sales, pricing, validation, legal/fiscal basics, and distribution.
- Contribution roles: feedback giver, event helper, mentor, speaker/opportunity connector. Many people are unsure how to contribute, so the product should make contribution explicit and low-friction.

## Invite Rollout Recommendation

1. Start with a small batch of active founders/builders with launched products, MVPs, revenue, or clear feedback intent.
2. Invite by source segment `Community Form` and keep the first wave manual enough to spot profile/consent issues.
3. After each batch, check activation success, profile completion, and whether people join a feedback/demo/local format.
4. Use the first accepted members to validate directory presentation before asking the `Forse` group to publish profiles.
